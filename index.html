About Me

I’m a third-year Physics undergraduate at Tsinghua University, currently part of an experimental nuclear physics research group. My research focuses on detector simulations and design for a polarised deuteron breakup experiment, which probes the nuclear symmetry energy. I study the performance of scintillators and simulate detector response using Geant4 to support the development of the polarimeter.

Alongside this, I have a strong passion for theoretical physics—especially quantum field theory and general relativity—which I continue to explore through independent reading and coursework. This website serves as a portfolio for my academic projects and technical writing.

Outside of academics, I’m a dedicated long-distance runner and a member of Tsinghua’s middle- and long-distance second team (中长跑二队). The team is made up of non-sports-specialist students who train seriously while balancing academic life. You can see a more detailed log of my training and achievements on my running page.

Projects & Writings

  • Canonical Quantization and Path Integral Quantization (正则量子化与路径积分量子化)

    A comparative essay on the formalisms of canonical quantization and Feynman's path integral quantization, discussing their equivalence and their respective advantages in different areas of quantum field theory.

  • A Brief Overview of Black Hole Thermodynamics

    An introductory overview of the four laws of black hole mechanics and their deep connection to the principles of classical thermodynamics, including concepts like Hawking radiation and Bekenstein-Hawking entropy.

  • Statistical Mechanics of the Quantum Harmonic Ensemble

    An analysis of a canonical ensemble of quantum harmonic oscillators, deriving thermodynamic properties from the partition function and comparing the utility of different representations.

  • Neutrino Mass and Oscillations (中微子质量与振荡)

    A review of the experimental evidence for neutrino flavour oscillations, exploring the Standard Model and the implications of non-zero neutrino mass for physics beyond the Standard Model.

  • The Action Principle in Sourceless Classical Electrodynamics

    A theoretical paper deriving Maxwell's equations in a vacuum from a relativistic Lagrangian density and the principle of stationary action.

  • Polarised Deuteron Breakup as a Probe of the Nuclear Symmetry Energy

    An exploration of how scattering experiments involving polarised deuterons can be used to constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, which is crucial for understanding neutron-rich matter in astrophysics.

  • The Complex Scalar Klein-Gordon Field (复标量克莱因–戈尔登场)

    This paper studies complex scalar Klein-Gordon fields starting from the action, deriving canonical momenta, commutation relations, and the Hamiltonian. It shows the theory describes two particles of the same mass, linked by a conserved U(1) charge. For two fields, the symmetry extends to U(2), yielding four conserved charges with an SU(2) structure. The framework generalizes to n fields, revealing a global SU(n) symmetry and corresponding conserved charges, highlighting the role of internal symmetries in quantum field theory.
