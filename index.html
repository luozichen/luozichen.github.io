About Me

I’m a third-year Physics undergraduate at Tsinghua University, currently part of an experimental nuclear physics research group. My research focuses on detector simulations and design for a polarised deuteron breakup experiment, which probes the nuclear symmetry energy. I study the performance of scintillators and simulate detector response using Geant4 to support the development of the polarimeter.

Alongside this, I have a strong passion for theoretical physics—especially quantum field theory and general relativity—which I continue to explore through independent reading and coursework. This website serves as a portfolio for my academic projects and technical writing.

Outside of academics, I’m a dedicated long-distance runner and a member of Tsinghua’s middle- and long-distance second team (中长跑二队). The team is made up of non-sports-specialist students who train seriously while balancing academic life. You can see a more detailed log of my training and achievements on my running page.

Projects & Writings

  • Canonical Quantization and Path Integral Quantization (正则量子化与路径积分量子化)

    An accessible comparison of canonical operator quantisation and Feynman's path integral: derivations, example systems and pragmatic trade-offs, emphasising where each method is most useful in QFT.

  • A Brief Overview of Black Hole Thermodynamics

    Survey of the four laws of black hole mechanics, Bekenstein–Hawking entropy and Hawking radiation, with semiclassical examples and discussion of entropy's role in gravitational thermodynamics.

  • Statistical Mechanics of the Quantum Harmonic Ensemble

    Derives the partition function for a canonical ensemble of quantum harmonic oscillators across energy, position and momentum representations, computing thermodynamic observables and comparing representation-specific insights.

  • Neutrino Mass and Oscillations (中微子质量与振荡)

    Review of experimental evidence for neutrino flavour oscillations, mass and mixing phenomenology, simple mass models and implications for the Standard Model and minimal extensions.

  • The Action Principle in Sourceless Classical Electrodynamics

    Derives Maxwell’s equations in vacuum from a relativistic Lagrangian, discussing gauge freedom, conserved currents from Noether's theorem and implications for classical and quantum formulations.

  • Polarised Deuteron Breakup as a Probe of the Nuclear Symmetry Energy

    Explores polarised-deuteron scattering observables and polarimeter design, showing how measurements can constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy relevant for neutron-rich matter.

  • The Complex Scalar Klein-Gordon Field (复标量克莱因–戈尔登场)

    Starts from the action to derive canonical momenta and commutators for the complex Klein–Gordon field, explains the conserved U(1) charge and generalises to multiple fields and SU(n) structure.
