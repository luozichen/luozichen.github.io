About Me

Third-year physics undergraduate at Tsinghua, working on detector simulation and polarimeter development.

I’m a third-year Physics undergraduate at Tsinghua University, currently part of an experimental nuclear physics research group. My research focuses on detector simulations and design for a polarised deuteron breakup experiment, which probes the nuclear symmetry energy. I study the performance of scintillators and simulate detector response using Geant4 to support the development of the polarimeter.

Alongside this, I have a strong passion for theoretical physics—especially quantum field theory and general relativity—which I continue to explore through independent reading and coursework. This website serves as a portfolio for my academic projects and technical writing.

Outside of academics, I’m a dedicated long-distance runner and a member of Tsinghua’s middle- and long-distance second team (中长跑二队). The team is made up of non-sports-specialist students who train seriously while balancing academic life. You can see a more detailed log of my training and achievements on my running page.

Essays & Writings

A selection of essays and technical notes — not peer-reviewed publications.

Canonical Quantization and Path Integral Quantization (正则量子化与路径积分量子化)

Clear comparison of canonical operator quantisation and Feynman's path integral formalism. Derivations, examples and pragmatic trade-offs, emphasising circumstances where each method simplifies quantum field calculations.

A Brief Overview of Black Hole Thermodynamics

Survey of the four laws of black hole mechanics, Bekenstein–Hawking entropy and Hawking radiation, with semiclassical examples and discussion of entropy and information in gravitational thermodynamics.

Statistical Mechanics of the Quantum Harmonic Ensemble

Derives the partition function for a canonical ensemble of quantum harmonic oscillators across energy, position and momentum representations, computes thermodynamic observables and compares representation-specific insights.

Neutrino Mass and Oscillations (中微子质量与振荡)

Review of experimental evidence for neutrino flavour oscillations, mass and mixing formalism, simple phenomenological mass models, experimental constraints and implications for physics beyond the Standard Model.

The Action Principle in Sourceless Classical Electrodynamics

Derives Maxwell’s equations in vacuum from a relativistic Lagrangian, discusses gauge freedom, Noether currents and connections between classical and quantum electrodynamics formulations.

Polarised Deuteron Breakup as a Probe of the Nuclear Symmetry Energy

Investigates polarised-deuteron scattering observables and polarimeter design, demonstrating how targeted measurements constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy relevant for neutron-rich astrophysical matter.

The Complex Scalar Klein-Gordon Field (复标量克莱因–戈尔登场)

Begins with the action to derive canonical momenta and commutation relations for a complex Klein–Gordon field, explains the conserved U(1) charge and generalises to multiple fields with SU(n) structure.
