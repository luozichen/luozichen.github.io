About Me

I am a third-year undergraduate student pursuing a degree in Physics at Tsinghua University. My academic interests lie primarily in theoretical physics, with a focus on statistical mechanics, classical electrodynamics, and particle physics.

This website serves as a portfolio for my academic projects and technical writings. Outside of my studies, I am a dedicated long-distance runner.

Projects & Writings

  • 正则量子化与路径积分量子化 (Canonical Quantization and Path Integral Quantization)

    A comparative essay on the formalisms of canonical quantization and Feynman's path integral quantization, discussing their equivalence and their respective advantages in different areas of quantum field theory.

  • A Brief Overview of Black Hole Thermodynamics

    An introductory overview of the four laws of black hole mechanics and their deep connection to the principles of classical thermodynamics, including concepts like Hawking radiation and Bekenstein-Hawking entropy.

  • Statistical Mechanics of the Quantum Harmonic Ensemble

    An analysis of a canonical ensemble of quantum harmonic oscillators, deriving thermodynamic properties from the partition function and comparing the utility of different representations.

  • Neutrino Mass and Oscillations (中微子质量与振荡)

    A review of the experimental evidence for neutrino flavour oscillations, exploring the PMNS matrix and the implications of non-zero neutrino mass for physics beyond the Standard Model.

  • The Action Principle in Sourceless Classical Electrodynamics

    A theoretical paper deriving Maxwell's equations in a vacuum from a relativistic Lagrangian density and the principle of stationary action ($ \delta S = 0 $).

  • Polarised Deuteron Breakup as a Probe of the Nuclear Symmetry Energy

    An exploration of how scattering experiments involving polarised deuterons can be used to constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, which is crucial for understanding neutron-rich matter in astrophysics.
