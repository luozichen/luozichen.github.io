Hi, I'm Zichen Luo (罗子辰). Born and raised in London, UK, I have been deeply fascinated by physics and the natural world since my early childhood. At the age of 14, I received an offer to join the undergraduate physics program at Tsinghua University in Beijing. Moving across the world to pursue this passion has been an incredible journey, immersing me in the rich academic environment of one of China's most historic and rigorous physics departments.

Currently, I am in my fourth and final year of study, completing my bachelor's thesis in experimental nuclear physics under the guidance of Prof. Xiao Zhigang. My research focuses on the development of polarization detectors for deuteron breakup experiments. This combines Geant4 Monte Carlo simulations with hands-on hardware design, including the assembly and testing of plastic scintillator arrays, to explore the isovector nature of nuclear symmetry energy.

Beyond the laboratory, my academic curiosities extend to the mathematical beauty of theoretical physics; I dedicate much of my independent study to Quantum Field Theory and General Relativity. This website is a simple, quiet corner of the internet where I host my academic notes, catalog my competitive running life as a member of Tsinghua's long-distance team, and share my occasional machine learning experiments. Thank you for visiting.

Current Research & Thesis

My academic work centers on experimental nuclear physics under the guidance of Prof. Xiao Zhigang’s research group. The key focus of my thesis project is:

  • Polarization Detectors: Development and construction of high-precision plastic scintillator arrays for deuteron breakup experiments to probe the isovector nature of nuclear symmetry energy.
  • Simulation Frameworks: Leveraging Geant4 Monte Carlo simulations to optimize scintillator geometry, light transport efficiency, and multi-particle coincidence logic.
